Short Bio

I am currently a Professor in the School of Computer Science and Technology at Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST). I previously worked as a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Computer Science at the University of British Columbia (UBC) and a Research Fellow in the School of Computing at the National University of Singapore (NUS). I received my Ph.D. from Nanyang Technological University (NTU), under the supervision of Prof. Xiaokui Xiao and Prof. Aixin Sun. Before that, I got my B.Eng. from Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST). I am a long-term visiting scholar of the AI Group of Prof. Pietro Liò at University of Cambridge.

Research Interests

My research is centered on the domains of data management & analysis, and machine learning. My work to date has been dedicated to developing scalable, efficient, and theoretically robust algorithms to enhance both the efficiency and effectiveness of graph analytics and graph learning. In graph learning, I have a particular interest in designing scalable and effective Graph Neural Networks. I also study strategies to optimize the ensemble of Large Language Models, focusing on improving their cost-efficiency and overall performance with guarantees. More recently, I have started exploring AI for Science, with a particular interest in data-driven and learning-based methods for scientific discovery.
